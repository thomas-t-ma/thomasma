About Me

I’m a Computer Science student at Emory University preparing for a career in medicine. I’m passionate about applying computational models to improve diagnostics, treatment decisions, and overall patient outcomes.

My research focuses on artificial intelligence in healthcare. I’m currently working with the Department of Pathology and Laboratory Medicine at Emory University Hospital on a deep learning project using flow cytometry data for multiple myeloma detection. I’m also developing a framework that leverages graph neural networks and attention-based mechanisms to predict breast cancer recurrence from whole-slide imaging.

I’m excited by the intersection of computer science and medicine and aim to contribute to a future where machine learning enhances clinical decision-making and personalized care.
